Definitions

Before signing on the dotted line, read through the definitions of commonly used words in your policy.

Death

Death occurring within 12 calendar months from the accident.

You/your

Any person whose name is stated in the schedule under the heading “insured persons

Bodily injury

Bodily injury caused by violent, accidental, external and visible means.

Accident

An accident is an unexpected and unintentional event, caused by violent, external and visible means, commonly leading to injury and/or resulting in hospitalisation, disablement or death following an accident

Permanent disablement

permanent disablement, as described in the compensation scale, occurring within twelve calendar months after sustaining the bodily injury.

Temporary total disability

Any person whose name is stated in the schedule under the heading “insured persons

Home and Contents

Buildings

Your house and outbuildings and boundary walls of which the construction and an address has been noted in the schedule.

Outbuildings

Attached or detached rooms and garages, which do not inter-lead to your house, situated at the same address as your house.

Use

The purpose of the asset. is it a private residence or used for conducting business.

Contents

Household goods and personal possessions that are inside your house and which belong to you or any other member of your family who normally live with you. Goods and possessions in your outbuildings are not covered.

Territorial limits

The area in which your cover is applicable. in this case its within the borders of the republic of south Africa

Insured perils

Fire, lightning, explosion,Theft,Storm, wind, water, hail, snow, flood.

Legal Expense Insurance

You/your

The insured name in the schedule, the insured’s spouse and their children.

Children

Natural, legally adopted or step-children under the age of 21 years, as well as children over the age of 21 years who are mentally or physically disabled or totally dependent on the insured or spouse and live with the insured. Children who are dependent on the insured or spouse and who study full time are included up to the age of 25 years. Married children are not included.

Legal costs and legal expenses

Legal costs and legal expenses, taxed in accordance with the rate applicable, incurred by an attorney who is appointed to represent you.

Legal action

legal procedure resulting in a matter being adjudicated in court by a way of action procedure. This excludes ex-parte application procedure unless it is an integral or necessary part of an action procedure.

Business or occupation

it is any form of labour and/or enterprise that generates regular income to the insured. This includes formal or informal employment as a part-time or full-time occupation. Business includes home based enterprise, property leasing and rental, after hours occupation and contractual business agreements.

Business legal

You/your/yours

Means the organisation/institution named in the Schedule.

Us/our/we

Means Santam Limited.

Legal costs and legal expenses

Means costs and expenses for which you are liable, including costs incurred by the attorney. We will only compensate you according to our tariff rates and for service providers, fees, and expenses we have approved.

Renewal period

Means a period of 12 consecutive months as shown in the Schedule.

Renewal date

Means the first day of a period of 12 consecutive months as shown in the Schedule.

Claim(s)

Means any request for compensation (indemnity), whether or not any amounts have been established for the claim(s)

SMME

Means Small, Medium and Micro Enterprises as defined and stated in the National Small Business Act 102 of 1996 as amended from time to time.

Schedule

Means the annexure forming part of this policy.

Civil legal action

Means an institution and/or defence of legal action procedure by way of summons.

Licence

Means a valid driver’s licence in compliance with legislation of the specific country where the vehicle is used at the time of any loss or damage. A person who is learning to drive must comply with legislation concerning learner drivers.

Micro SME

Annual period

Means a period of 12 consecutive months as shown in the Schedule. The first annual period starts on the inception date of the policy shown in the Schedule up to the revision date shown in the Schedule.Subsequent annual periods are the 12 consecutive months between revision dates.

Armed robbery

Means the unlawful taking of money in your possession by force or intimidation making use of a dangerous weapon

Burglary

Means the unlawful taking of your property by someone breaking into the building where your business is situated and there are actual, visible signs of break-in. Our compensation for burglary is limited to the amount shown in the Schedule.

Business

Means the nature of your business as shown in the Schedule.

Business interruption

Means the gross profits you lost after your property was damaged due to an insured event. You must prove that the gross profit of the business has reduced as a result of an insured event. We will not pay more than the package limit as shown in the Schedule. We will only start paying for your gross profits three days after your property was damaged or lost.

Claim/claims

Means any request for us to pay you for loss or damage.

Contents

means the contents of your business at the risk address . Contents include office equipment, stock, materials in trade, tools, machinery, glass, money and electronic equipment. It also includes business interruption following certain insured events. Contents do not include stamps, manuscripts, samples, designs, patterns, models, moulds or any vehicles.

Excess

Means the amount you have to pay before we settle any claim.

Electronic equipment

Means electronic devices or machines for example computers and laptops. We will not pay for electronic equipment if it fails to do what it is supposed to do. We will not pay to load your programmes again or to reload any information.

Fire

Means damage caused by flame. We will also pay the municipality or fire brigade when they help you to fight the fire.

Glass

Means any glass at the risk address which belongs to you or for which you are responsible.

Gross profit

Means any glass at the risk address which belongs to you or for which you are responsible.

Inception date

Means the date your policy cover started. The inception date is shown in the Schedule.

Machinery

Means a system or device for doing work together with its power source and auxiliary equipment.

Malicious damage

Means damage caused by someone with a clear intention to cause this damage. You may not damage your own property. We will pay the amount shown in the Schedule.

Money

Means cash, coins or cheques.

Net profit

Means the net trading profit before the deduction of any tax payable on profits.

Period of insurance

Means one calendar month from the inception date and continues for as long as you pay your premium and stops when you or we cancel this policy.

Product

Means any property (including containers and labels) which has been designed, specified, formulated, manufactured, constructed, installed, sold, supplied, distributed, treated, serviced, altered or repaired by you or on your behalf, after it has left your custody or control.

Revision date

Means the first day of a period of 12 consecutive months as shown in the Schedule.

Risk address

Means the address of the premises on which your business is situated and/or operating from. Your risk address is shown in the Schedule.

Schedule

Means the document containing your unique policy details and forming part of this policy.

Stock

Means the goods you sell to your customers. We will pay you the market value of your stock. Market value is the amount it will cost to buy new stock.

Us/our/we

Means Santam Limited.

Working expenses

Means your expenses that you still have to pay even though you cannot conduct your business due to an insured event. E.g. you still have to pay your employees, your water, electricity and cellphone accounts even though your business is closed due to an insured event.

You/your/yours

Means the names shown in the Schedule whether it is your individual name or the name of your business
